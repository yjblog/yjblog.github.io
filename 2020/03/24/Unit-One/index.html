Thirty Words

Part One

  1. authority [əˈθɔːrəti]

    • The judge(法官) had no authority to order a second trial(审判).

  2. demonstrate [ˈdemənstreɪt]

    • Please demonstrate how the machine works.

  3. challenge [ˈtʃælɪndʒ]

    • This career offers a challenge.

  4. neglect [nɪˈɡlekt]

    • No country can afford to neglect education.

  5. alter [ˈɔːltər]

    • Circumstances alter cases.

    • Difficult circumstances(environment) can temper(hone磨练) one’s will(意志).

  6. abuse [əˈbjuːs]

    • I didn’t expect him to descend(decline) to personal abuse.(人身攻击)

  7. disguise [dɪsˈɡaɪz]

    • She could not disguise her amusement at his mistake.

  8. deny [dɪˈnaɪ]

    • No one can deny the fact that fire burns.

  9. manual [ˈmænjuəl]

    • Work done by machines has replaced.

  10. constant [ˈkɑːnstənt]

    • This has been a timely reminder of the need for constant care.

Part Two

  1. contradict [ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkt]
    • Don’t contradict your father.
  2. expand [ɪkˈspænd]
    • Metals expand when they are heated.
  3. straightforward [ˌstreɪtˈfɔːrwərd]
    • I must insist on you give me a straightforward answer.
  4. principle [ˈprɪnsəpl]
    • The system works on the principle that heat rises.
  5. enthusiasm [ɪnˈθuːziæzəm]
    • His enthusiasm breathed new life into the company.
  6. decline [dɪˈklaɪn]
    • She is on the decline and may die soon.
  7. aggressive [əˈɡresɪv]
    • He has an aggressive manner.
  8. propose [prəˈpoʊz]
    • Did he propose to you?
  9. brand [brænd]
    • These frightful experences are branded on his memory.
  10. harsh [hɑːrʃ]
    • His harsh word caused her much pain.

Part Three

  1. commit [kəˈmɪt]
    • One cannot commit crimes with impunity(/ɪmˈpjuːnəti/不受惩罚).
  2. obscure [əbˈskjʊr]
    • Her poetry is full of obscure literary allusions.
  3. clarity [ˈklærəti]
    • The clarity of the mountain air.
  4. profound [prəˈfaʊnd]
    • The teacher had both extensive knowledge and profound scholarship.
  5. absorb [əbˈzɔːrb,əbˈsɔːrb]
    • Plants can absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen.
  6. privilege [ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ]
    • It was a privilege to work with him.
  7. justify [ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪ]
    • The end justifies the means.
  8. substitute [ˈsʌbstɪtuːt]
    • The coach substituted Smith for Jones.
  9. evil [ˈiːvl,ˈiːvɪl]
    • His ecil designs were frustrated.
  10. stereotype [ˈsteriətaɪp]
    • He doesn’t conform to the usual stereotype of the city businessman with adark suit and rolled umbrella.
